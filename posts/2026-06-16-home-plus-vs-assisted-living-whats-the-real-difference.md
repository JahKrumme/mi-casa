When families start researching elder care options in Kansas, two terms come up frequently: assisted living and Home Plus. They sound similar, and both provide support for seniors who need daily help, but the experience of living in each one is very different.

### Size Is the Biggest Difference

Assisted living facilities are typically large operations, sometimes housing dozens or even hundreds of residents across multiple wings or floors. Staff rotate between many residents throughout the day, and the environment often feels more institutional, even when operators work hard to make it feel welcoming.

A Home Plus facility is an actual house. Kansas law limits Home Plus homes to a small, intimate setting, which means residents live alongside just a handful of neighbors, not a crowd. Staff know everyone by name. Meals happen around a real dining table. The rhythm of the day feels like a household, not a schedule.

### The Setting Matters More Than You Think

Most people picture elder care as a building with long hallways, a nurses station, and a common room with a television. That is what large assisted living facilities look like in practice.

At Mi Casa Care Homes, residents live in a real house in a real neighborhood. There is a kitchen where meals are cooked fresh, a living room where people gather, a patio for warm Kansas evenings, and private bedrooms that residents can make their own. It does not feel like a facility because it is not one.

### Cost and Payment Options

Large assisted living facilities tend to carry higher price tags, in part because of the overhead involved in running a large operation. Home Plus facilities are often more affordable, and in Kansas, Home Plus homes can accept KanCare, the state Medicaid program, making them accessible to families across a wider range of financial situations. Long term care insurance and private pay are also accepted.

### Which Is the Right Fit?

Home Plus tends to be a great fit for seniors who value personal relationships, thrive in small group settings, and want to feel like a member of a household rather than a resident of a facility. If your loved one does well in quieter, familiar environments and would benefit from staff who truly know them, a Home Plus home is worth a serious look.

Assisted living may be a better fit for someone who needs a very high level of medical care, wants a wide range of on-site amenities, or prefers a larger social environment with more organized programming.
