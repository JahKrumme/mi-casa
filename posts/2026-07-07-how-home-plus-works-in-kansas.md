A **Home Plus facility** is one option many Kansas families may not know about at first. It is a licensed care setting that provides support in a smaller, more home-like environment. Instead of a large senior living campus, a Home Plus facility is usually located in a residential-style home with a smaller number of residents.

For families in Wichita, Towanda, and surrounding Kansas communities, understanding how Home Plus works can make it easier to choose the right level of care.

## What Is a Home Plus Facility in Kansas?

In Kansas, a Home Plus facility is a type of adult care home. Kansas law defines Home Plus as a residence or facility that cares for **not more than 12 individuals** who are not related to the operator or owner and who need personal care and may need supervised nursing care because of functional impairment. 

Put more simply, Home Plus is designed for adults who need help with daily life but may not need the higher medical level of care provided in a nursing home.

A resident in a Home Plus setting may need help with things like:

* Bathing, dressing, grooming, and mobility
* Medication management
* Meals and nutrition
* Daily routines and supervision
* Safety monitoring
* Support from trained caregivers
* Nursing oversight, depending on the resident’s needs

The goal is to provide care in a setting that feels more like a home than an institution.

## Who Regulates Home Plus Facilities in Kansas?

Home Plus facilities in Kansas are licensed and regulated through the **Kansas Department for Aging and Disability Services**, also known as KDADS. KDADS oversees adult care home licensing, certification, inspections, and related regulatory information for facilities across the state. 

This matters because families should not have to guess whether a care home is operating appropriately. A licensed Home Plus facility is expected to follow Kansas requirements related to resident care, health and safety, staffing, medication practices, and resident rights.

Families can also use the KDADS Adult Care Home Directory to look up adult care homes, including Home Plus facilities, and review available facility information. 

## How Is Home Plus Different from Assisted Living?

Home Plus and assisted living can sound similar because both provide support outside of a person’s private home. The biggest difference is usually the **size and environment**.

Assisted living communities are often larger buildings with many residents, apartment-style units, shared dining areas, activity rooms, and more staff departments. A Home Plus facility is much smaller and is designed to feel like a residential home.

For many families, the appeal of Home Plus is the smaller setting. Residents may share meals around a table, see the same caregivers more often, and follow a daily routine that feels familiar and less overwhelming.

A larger assisted living community may be a good fit for someone who wants more amenities, social programming, and independence. A Home Plus setting may be a better fit for someone who needs more daily support, benefits from consistency, or feels more comfortable in a quieter home environment.

## How Is Home Plus Different from a Nursing Home?

A nursing home generally provides a higher level of medical care than a Home Plus facility. Nursing homes are often used for people who need skilled nursing care, more intensive medical monitoring, rehabilitation services, or complex care after hospitalization.

Home Plus can be a good middle-ground option for someone who needs more help than they can safely receive at home but does not necessarily need a skilled nursing facility.

A Home Plus resident may need help with daily activities, medication management, supervision, and a structured environment. However, someone who needs around-the-clock skilled nursing interventions, intensive rehabilitation, or complex medical treatment may require a nursing home or another higher level of care.

## What Kind of Care Is Provided in a Home Plus Setting?

The exact services can vary by facility and by each resident’s care plan, but Home Plus care commonly includes support with activities of daily living. These are the everyday tasks that can become difficult with aging, illness, memory changes, or mobility concerns.

Care may include:

* Personal care assistance
* Medication reminders or medication administration
* Meals and snacks
* Laundry and housekeeping
* Mobility support
* Monitoring for safety
* Family communication
* Social interaction and daily routine
* Nursing oversight when appropriate

Because Home Plus homes are smaller, care can often feel more personal. Staff are more likely to learn each resident’s habits, preferences, routines, and personality.

That familiarity can make a major difference for older adults who feel anxious, confused, or overwhelmed in larger care settings.

## Who May Be a Good Fit for Home Plus?

A Home Plus facility may be a good fit for an older adult who:

* Is no longer safe living alone
* Needs help with bathing, dressing, grooming, or moving around
* Needs support managing medications
* Benefits from a calm and structured routine
* Prefers a smaller, home-like environment
* Needs caregiver support throughout the day
* Has family members who want more consistent communication
* May have early or moderate memory concerns, depending on the facility’s ability to meet those needs

Every person’s situation is different. The best way to know whether Home Plus is the right fit is to talk honestly about the person’s care needs, safety concerns, medical history, personality, and daily routine.

## Why Families in Kansas Choose Home Plus

Families often consider Home Plus because they want care that feels personal. A smaller setting can make it easier for residents to feel known, not just managed.

Some families choose Home Plus because their loved one does not do well in large facilities. Others are looking for a place that feels closer to a real home, with shared meals, familiar caregivers, and a quieter daily rhythm.

For families in Wichita, Towanda, and nearby Kansas communities, Home Plus can offer a balance between safety, support, and comfort.

## Questions to Ask Before Choosing a Home Plus Facility

Before choosing a Home Plus facility in Kansas, families should ask clear questions during a tour or phone call.

Helpful questions include:

* Is the facility currently licensed as a Home Plus facility in Kansas?
* How many residents live in the home?
* What is the caregiver-to-resident ratio?
* What types of care needs can the facility safely support?
* How are medications managed?
* Is nursing oversight available?
* How does the facility communicate with families?
* What does a typical day look like?
* Are meals prepared in the home?
* What happens if a resident’s care needs increase?
* What payment options are accepted?
* Can families visit the home before making a decision?

These questions help families compare options and understand whether the home can truly meet their loved one’s needs.
