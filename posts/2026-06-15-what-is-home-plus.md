If you're exploring care options for a loved one in Kansas, you may have come across the term "Home Plus." It sounds simple -- and in many ways it is -- but families often aren't sure exactly what it means, how it differs from assisted living or a nursing home, or who it's really designed for.

This guide covers what you need to know about Home Plus facilities in Kansas: how they work, how they're regulated, how they compare to other care settings, and how to know whether one might be the right fit for your family.

## What Is a Home Plus Facility?

A Home Plus facility is a licensed residential care setting in Kansas that provides personal care and nursing support for adults who can no longer live independently. The name captures what makes these settings distinctive: they function as genuine homes -- not as institutions or clinical environments.

Home Plus facilities operate in residential buildings -- real houses in real neighborhoods -- with a small number of residents. Unlike a hospital wing or a large assisted living campus, a Home Plus home looks and feels like what it is: a place where people live.

All Home Plus facilities in Kansas are licensed and regulated by the Kansas Department for Aging and Disability Services (KDADS), which sets standards for staffing, safety, medication management, and care quality.

## How Is Home Plus Regulated in Kansas?

KDADS licenses and inspects all Home Plus facilities in Kansas. The department establishes requirements for:

- The number of residents a facility may serve
- Staff qualifications and training requirements
- Health, safety, and sanitation standards
- Medication management and nursing oversight
- Residents' rights and individualized care planning

KDADS conducts regular inspections to ensure facilities meet these standards. Families can look up licensed facilities and learn more about Home Plus requirements at [kdads.ks.gov](https://kdads.ks.gov).

## How Is Home Plus Different from Assisted Living?

This is one of the first questions families ask, and it's a fair one. At first glance, both seem similar: adults receive personal care in a setting that isn't their own home. But there are real and meaningful differences.

**Size:** Assisted living communities in Kansas can range from dozens to hundreds of residents housed in large buildings with wings, floors, elevators, and shared amenities. Home Plus facilities, by contrast, serve a much smaller number of residents in a setting that is literally a house in a neighborhood.

**Environment:** Home Plus residents live in a space that looks and feels domestic -- a kitchen, a living room, shared dining at a table, and often a backyard or front porch. Assisted living facilities are typically designed to serve many people efficiently, which often means a more institutional feel: carpeted hallways, numbered rooms, centralized dining rooms.

**Meals:** In a Home Plus home, meals are typically prepared fresh in the home's kitchen and shared together at the table. In larger facilities, food is often served cafeteria-style or delivered from a commercial kitchen.

**Relationships:** Because the setting is small, staff in a Home Plus facility genuinely get to know each resident -- their preferences, routines, stories, and personalities. In a larger facility, a resident may interact with many different caregivers over the course of a week, with less consistency and familiarity.

## How Is Home Plus Different from a Nursing Home?

Nursing homes provide a higher level of medical care than Home Plus facilities. They are designed for individuals who require round-the-clock skilled nursing services -- often after a major medical event, surgery, or serious illness.

A Home Plus facility is appropriate for adults who need:

- Help with daily activities such as bathing, dressing, grooming, and getting around
- Medication management and regular nursing oversight
- A safe, structured, homelike living environment

But who do not need:

- Intensive medical monitoring around the clock
- Skilled nursing interventions following surgery or hospitalization
- Short-term rehabilitation services

For many families, Home Plus represents a meaningful middle ground: more support than staying at home alone, and more personal and homelike than a traditional nursing facility.

## Who Is a Good Fit for a Home Plus Setting?

Home Plus tends to be a good fit for adults who:

- Need regular help with activities of daily living (bathing, dressing, grooming, mobility)
- Require medication management or nursing oversight
- Benefit from a calm, familiar, structured daily routine
- Are experiencing age-related decline, including early to moderate memory loss or dementia
- Would thrive with consistent relationships with a small, familiar group of caregivers

Every family's situation is different, and the right setting depends on a person's specific care needs, personality, and what will allow them to live with the greatest dignity and quality of life. A visit to a Home Plus home is often the best way to know whether it's the right fit.

## Questions to Ask When Evaluating a Home Plus Facility

If you're considering a Home Plus facility for a loved one, these questions are worth raising during a visit:

- How many residents does the facility currently serve?
- What is the staff-to-resident ratio on a typical day?
- What does a typical day look like for residents?
- How is medication managed, and who administers it?
- How does the facility communicate with families?
- Is the facility currently licensed by KDADS? When was the last inspection?
- What payment options are accepted -- Medicaid (HCBS), long-term care insurance, private pay?
