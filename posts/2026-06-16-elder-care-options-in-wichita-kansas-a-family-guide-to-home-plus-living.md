Finding the right care for an aging parent or loved one is one of the most important decisions a family can make. For families in Wichita, Towanda, and the surrounding areas of Butler and Sedgwick County, there is an option that many people have never heard of — and it might be exactly what they are looking for.

### What Elder Care Options Exist in Wichita?

Families in the Wichita area typically consider a few different paths when a loved one needs daily support:

* **In-home care**

   — a caregiver comes to the family home to help with daily tasks
* **Assisted living**

   — a larger residential facility with staff and programming
* **Nursing homes**

   — a higher level of medical care for those with significant health needs
* **Home Plus facilities**

   — a licensed residential care home serving a small, intimate setting in a real house in a real neighborhood

Most families are familiar with the first three. Home Plus is the option that often gets overlooked, even though it is frequently the best fit.

### What Makes Home Plus Different in Kansas

Kansas is one of a handful of states that licenses a specific category of small residential care homes called Home Plus facilities. These homes are regulated by the Kansas Department for Aging and Disability Services, known as KDADS, and are designed to provide personal care and supervised nursing support in a genuine home environment.

Unlike large assisted living facilities, a Home Plus home in Kansas operates out of an actual house in a residential neighborhood. Residents share meals, have their own private space, and are cared for by staff who know them personally. It is a level of familiarity and warmth that larger facilities simply cannot replicate.

### Who Is a Good Fit for Home Plus Care in Kansas?

Home Plus care tends to work especially well for:

* Seniors who thrive in small, familiar environments
* Adults who want personal relationships with their caregivers
* Families who want staff to truly know their loved one
* Those who qualify for KanCare, Kansas Medicaid, which is accepted at Home Plus facilities
* Families looking for a more affordable alternative to large assisted living
